About Me

I always had an interest in technology and gadgets. So, when I went to college, Computer Science seemed like a natural fit for me. I started with Java and never looked back. I graduated in 2007 from the University of South Carolina at Aiken with honors and a degree in Math and Computer Science.

At that point, I wanted to continue my education. So, I decided to pursue a master's degree in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of South Carolina at Columbia. It was one of the best decisions that I have ever made. I learned so much in those two years that made me a better developer and computer scientist in general. I graduated in 2009 with a M.E. in Computer Science and Engineering.

During my summers, I interned for the Department of Energy at the Savannah River Site near my home in Aiken, SC. After I graduated in 2009, they wanted to keep me around and offered me a job with the Desktop Systems Team.

This was a new experience for me, going from mainly being a developer to being a systems administrator. However, I believe being a developer has helped me to also be a great systems administrator because I can look at a problem from a variety of angles. I've had to learn a lot of skills and fill a lot of roles including developer, group policy expert, patch manager, and too many others to name. I still get to do quite a bit of development in various languages including C, C++, Perl, JavaScript, Python, Powershell, VBScript, and others. Currently, I'm the System Center Configuration Manager administrator. As the SCCM admin, I perform a lot of tasks including software update configuration and deployment, operating system configuration and deployment, and report management. One of the things that I'm most proud of is that I designed and implemented an operating system deployment process that migrated almost 9000 PC's from Windows XP to Windows 7.

Tools

I generally prefer open source or freeware tools and want to highlight some of them.

My preferred text editor on Windows is Notepad++ and KATE on Linux. Currently, my favorite Linux distribution is Mint. I also prefer to have some Linux tools available on Windows so I will either install MSYS or use GNU on Windows (GOW). Most of the time, I do not use and IDE, but when I do, I will use SharpDevelop, CodeBlocks, or Visual Studio.

As far as languages, I look at whatever the task or project is and try to pick the language that best fits.
